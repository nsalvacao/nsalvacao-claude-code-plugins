# Phase 1 — Opportunity and Portfolio Framing Essentials

## What to Do
- Frame the opportunity: define the problem, expected outcome, and AI justification in an Opportunity Statement
- Assess technical, data, and AI feasibility at high level; identify blockers early
- Map key stakeholders and their engagement status
- Build the initial risk register and Value Hypothesis
- Present to governance for funding and portfolio entry decision

## Who
| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Product Owner / Sponsor | Own Opportunity Statement and Value Hypothesis |
| PM | Stakeholder Map, Initial Risk Note, Funding Recommendation |
| Engineering Lead | Early Feasibility Note |
| Data Lead / ML Lead | AI/Data Feasibility Note |
| Governance Board | Portfolio Decision Record sign-off |

## Key Skills
- `gate-checklist` — use when preparing the Gate A review and validating evidence
- `risk-management` — use when building the initial risk register in subfase 1.2
- `artefact-authoring` — use when creating Opportunity Statement, Value Hypothesis, or Feasibility Notes
- `ai-lifecycle` — use when assessing AI/data feasibility and justifying AI approach

## Entry Criteria
- A problem or opportunity has been identified by a business sponsor or product owner

## Exit Criteria
- Portfolio Decision Record signed off; Gate A passed; project formally funded and in portfolio
